Prof. Yuyu Chen is a Boya Distinguished Professor at Peking University and the Director of the Institute of Economic Policy Research at Peking University. He received his Ph.D. in Economics from the Australian National University in 2002. Prior to that, he worked at the Department of Macroeconomic Management of the State Commission for Restructuring the Economic System of China.
His research interests include economic development and productivity, human capital and growth, health and pollution, behavioral economics and labor markets, income distribution, and regional disparities. His work has been published in leading academic journals such as the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS), Journal of Political Economy, American Economic Review, Review of Economics and Statistics, Journal of Human Resources, Journal of Health Economics, Journal of Environmental Economics and Management, Economic Development and Cultural Change, Explorations in Economic History, and Population Studies.
He has received multiple awards for outstanding achievements in social science research from the Ministry of Education and has been recognized with the Guanghua School of Management Teaching Excellence Award multiple times. In 2014, he was awarded the National Science Fund for Distinguished Young Scholars. In 2017, he was selected as a Changjiang Distinguished Professor by the Ministry of Education. In the 2013-2014 academic year, he received Peking University's Teaching Excellence Award.
