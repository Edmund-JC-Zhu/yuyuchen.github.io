Professor Yuyu Chen is a Boya Distinguished Professor at Guanghua School of Management, Peking University and the Director of the Institute of Economic Policy Research at Peking University.
His research interests include economic development and productivity, human capital and growth, health and pollution, behavioral economics and labor markets, income distribution, and regional disparities. His work has been published in leading academic journals such as the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS), Journal of Political Economy, American Economic Review, Review of Economic Studies, Review of Economics and Statistics, Journal of Human Resources, Journal of Health Economics, Journal of Environmental Economics and Management, Economic Development and Cultural Change, Explorations in Economic History, and Population Studies.
Professor Chen has been actively engaged in teaching at all levels, including undergraduate, graduate, MBA, and EMBA programs, as well as advising Ph.D. students. He currently teaches Principles of Economics, Labor Economics, and Macroeconomics.
He received his Ph.D. in Economics from the Australian National University in 2002. Prior to that, he worked at the Department of Macroeconomic Management of the State Commission for Restructuring the Economic System of China.
